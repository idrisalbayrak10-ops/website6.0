Audience War

To understand who to sell your cartoon to, you must first understand who has the audience. Although Netflix led the revolution, the market is now fragmented.

Netflixis the volumetric leader for "Adult Animation." Amazon is a sleeping giant; Disney+ is a "Walled Garden" mostly closed to external IPs (Intellectual Property).

💡 Insight: Netflix has the highest "churn" rate, making them the most likely buyer for fresh independent IPs.

Show the Money

1. Cost + Premium (Netflix)

100% of costs + 20% profit. You make money instantly, but you lose the IP (ownership).

2. License Fee (Amazon/Hulu)

Fixed fee to rent for 1-3 years. You keep the IP, but you take the production risk.

3. Revenue Share (AVOD)

They pay pennies per view. There is no money upfront. Archive is good for content.

The Road to Agreement

You cannot upload a PDF to Netflix.com. To get a contract, you must overcome the "Gatekeepers".

Strategic Advice

It's nearly impossible for a new studio without representation to go directly to Netflix or Disney.

Your Best Move: Prepare a "Promo Video" (1-2 min) and a powerful Pitch Bible. For Amazon/Tubi access FilmHub approach aggregators like or have a look at animation markets (Annecy) Sales Representative find it.

Getting Started Checklist:

  • Determine Target Audience (Child 6-11 vs Adult)
  • Produce "Proof of Concept"
  • List the Top 10 Sales Representatives in Your Type
  • Allocate a budget for legal advice
